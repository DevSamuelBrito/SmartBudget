# Product

## Register

brand

This document is written for the new public landing page (`feat/landing-page`), the one brand-register surface in an otherwise product-register app. The core app — dashboard, transactions, budgets, reports — stays product register; treat it under that register for any task scoped to those screens.

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary audience is budget-conscious individuals — students and young professionals — trying to get control of day-to-day spending for the first time. They likely have no budgeting history beyond a bank app or a spreadsheet they abandoned, and they're evaluating SmartBudget in a moment of "I need to stop overspending," not out of enthusiasm for finance tools. The landing page meets them there: skeptical of another finance app, wary of jargon, and unwilling to sign up before seeing what the product actually does.

## Product Purpose

SmartBudget is a personal finance tracker: transactions, customizable categories, monthly budget limits with real-time spending indicators, a visual dashboard, and PDF/Excel report exports, in English and Brazilian Portuguese. The landing page's job is narrower — convert a first-time visitor into a free-tier signup by demonstrating the product in action before asking for anything. Success is a visitor who watches or explores the demo and creates a free account with a clear idea of what they just signed up for.

## Positioning

Real budget control, not passive tracking: category-level spending limits with real-time indicators that stop overspending before it happens, instead of a dashboard that just reports it afterward the way spreadsheets and most trackers do.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: see the product in action first — a demo, walkthrough, or live screenshots — before any signup ask.
- Secondary CTA: create a free account, offered as a soft, low-pressure ask alongside or right after the demo, not competing with it.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: the budget app that stops you before you overspend, not after.
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a serious, well-built product — secure, tested, actively maintained; (2) it does more than a spreadsheet or a passive tracker — real limits, real reports; (3) it's easy to start today, for free.
- Proof on hand: none real yet. Ship with placeholder proof content (e.g. a stand-in stat or quote block) clearly structured so it's a drop-in swap later — do not fabricate specific numbers, names, or quotes as if real. Open-source credibility signals already available in the repo (CI passing, SonarCloud quality gate, Snyk scan) are fair game as a trust marker in place of testimonials.

## Brand Personality

Modern and energetic: confident, fast-moving, optimistic. Budgeting is framed as an active habit worth doing, not a chore or a source of anxiety. Approachable over corporate — plain language, no intimidation — without tipping into cutesy or gimmicky.

## Anti-references

Not generic fintech: no navy-and-gold palette, no corporate stock photography of people pointing at laptops, no enterprise-dashboard chrome. That look reads as impersonal and at odds with the energetic, approachable tone this brand wants.

## Design Principles

Show, don't tell: the demo does the convincing before any signup ask, matching the primary CTA. Control, not just visibility: every claim ties back to preventing overspending, not merely visualizing it after the fact. Confidence without jargon: energetic and modern, but plain-spoken — finance shouldn't feel intimidating. Low-pressure conversion: signup stays a soft ask beside the product story, never gating it. Register boundary: this brand register describes the landing page only; the core app keeps its existing product-register conventions untouched.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: contrast ratios, full keyboard navigation, and `prefers-reduced-motion` support as the baseline, with no unusual requirements beyond established best practice.
